<b>PERTINENT INFORMATION:</b>

Good grief, where to start...

I&#39;m incredibly, elaborately imaginative. I spend 90 percent of every day thinking and LARP&#39;ing and this probably leads to a lot of the trouble I get in because, apparently, people don&#39;t like to be thought
about more than they think about you. Maybe they think it&#39;s unfair or casts them in a bad light.

Too damned bad. Anyway, I&#39;m quite happily from Michigan -- Detroit, at the moment, though I&#39;d like to live in Ann Arbor. If I can walk around lost at four am in the cold and not have a bad memory about it, then I think that&#39;s the place I&#39;d love to live. My other choice is Brooklyn, New York, but I haven&#39;t exactly decided.

For now, I&#39;m focusing on recovering from recent brain surgery and trying to get my academic life back on track so that I can get through undergraduate school and finally make it to medical school before, hopefully, doing a fellowship in pathology and becoming a forensic pathologist.

I&#39;d love to work full-time in a medical examiner&#39;s office either here in Michigan or in New York. Medicine, in general, though, has been a passion of mine since I was a child though I did commit the grave error of forgetting that fact once I reached high school.

Damned allure of screenwriting. If I could do both, I&#39;d be in Purgatory. Heaven or Hell would mean I&#39;m probably <i>really</i> dead and was either terribly good or awesomely bad, though given that I don&#39;t actually believe in the afterlife itself that&#39;d be a little difficult to achieve.

I&#39;m still figuring out the religion aspect of my life, but I find myself at home in both Catholicism and Judaism. Yes, they conflict. I&#39;ve spoken with a Sister about this so I&#39;m not that worried. I&#39;m only interested in what feels right for me, not what the rest of the world thinks most of the time.

If I could get rid of the lingering insecurity about it, I&#39;d feel a lot better, though.

I believe that&#39;ll come with time, however, so I suppose that&#39;d make me an optimist. I feel like a cynic most of the time but my own beliefs and thoughts consistently bely that so I&#39;m just going to go with it.

Whatever happens, happens. I&#39;ll get through it.
